Reflections

Hesed

Jesus Christ is Jehova, and He want's to form a relatinoship with us. From the beginning, Jehova has made covenants with God's children, and those covenants help us to create a connection with God and Christ that we couldn't have otherwise. Just like how having a relationship with someone you love without being married to them is different, when we enter into a covenant with God, we enjoy a special kind of love from God. Hesed is the Hebrew word for this special kind of love, but there is no English equivalent. Hesed is often translated in the JKV as loving kindness or mercy.

In Isaiah and throughout scripture, Jehova sometimes relates his relationship with us to a marriage relationship or a family relatinoship. I think comparing our relationship with Christ to a marriage relatinoship is really helpful. There is just something different about making a commitment to each other through a ceremony.

Repentance

The Book of Isaiah is full of examples of how Christ will never forget us and always help us come back to Him. Like with the Israelites, no matter how many times we turn from God and follow our own will, Christ will always help us come back to Him. Not only will He help us, but he is constantly preparing a way for us to come back to Him. Like with Lehi's posterity. Even though they strayed away from God for many years, God was preparing the Book of Mormon to bring them back into His fold thousands of years later. Christ wants as many peopel to return to Him as possible, but He won't force us to create a relationship with Him. Forcing something is not the way to create the connected relatinoship that Christ wants to have with us.

Prohpecy

I have learned a lot about prophecy through this class. Isaiah prophesied to help the Israelites of his time, but his prophecies also connected to events in the future. Sometimes Isaiah meant to prophesy of future events like Christ and the last days, but sometimes, he didn't intend to prophesy of the things we, as modern christians, often think he did. Even if all of Isaiah's prophecies weren't specifically targetted towards our day or Christ's day, we could still liken them to us like Nephi did so often. The principles that can be learned from Isaiah's prophecies are true principles that help us learn more about God and Jesus Christ. Here are some principles that I have learned from Isaiah's prophecies.

    God has our best interests in mind
    God knows more than us
    God keeps His promises
    God will use His covenant people to share the gospel
