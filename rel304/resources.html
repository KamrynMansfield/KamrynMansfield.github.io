Books

Learning to Love Isaiah by Kerry Muhlestein. Latter-day Saint author. Focused on the historical context.

The SBL Study Bible by Society of Biblical Literature. Study bibles like this can be a great investment for a deeper study.

Other Isaiah Commentaries. The Anchor Yale Bible Commentaries has some good commentaries.

Isaiah: A Prophets Prophet by Robert Miller. Latter-day Saint author. This guy doesn't focus on the historical context as much. He just focuses on how the application to today.

There are other good Isaiah Commentaries out there. They often are really thick and can sometimes go word by word commentaries. There are some good commentaries, but there are also some less good commentaries that are more opinionated than academic. One way that Dr. Seers likes to judge the commentaries by reading what they say about the end of Isaiah 5 where it talks about raising an ensign to the nations. Originally, Isaiah was not talking about latter-day missionaries. That could be an interpretation for today, but it was actually talking about how God would raise an ensign to tell the armies to come attack the Israelites.
